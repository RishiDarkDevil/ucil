1. **Large module-introduction commit** — `1f2c446` is 989 insertions (chunker.rs + lib.rs).  The workflow soft-target is ~50 lines/commit, with DEC-0001 / DEC-0005 permitting up to ~550 for module-introduction commits.  989 exceeds that ceiling, but matches in-crate precedent: `WO-0017` introduced `symbols.rs` in a single 1,152-line commit (`ba4b4bd`) and `WO-0018` introduced `tag_cache.rs` at 620 lines (`74fb79b`). 2. **Four commits omit the `Feature: P1-W2-F03` trailer** — `commit-style.md` specifies "every feature commit includes `Feature: <ID>`".  The two genuine feature-modifying commits (`1f2c446` — the chunker.rs introduction, `912f735` — the clippy/rustdoc fix) DO carry the trailer.  The four test-infrastructure commits (`0411b8a`, `1c69500`, `6201655`, `b86d69e`) intentionally omit it.  The rationale is explicit and plausible: `scripts/reality-check.sh` uses `git log --grep='Feature: <id>'` to build its CANDIDATES stash set, so a `Feature: P1-W2-F03` trailer on the safeguard file would cause the oracle to stash the safeguard itself and defeat its own check.  **Not a blocker** — the executor's commit bodies clearly document the workaround and the strict reading of the rule only requires the trailer on "feature commits" (i.e. feat/fix), both of which comply.  Verifier may choose to treat `commit-style.md` as "every `feat`/`fix` commit" and accept, OR require a planner ADR codifying the oracle-friendly commit-trailer carve-out.

3. **Three commits (0411b8a → 1c69500 → 6201655) are an intra-branch reshuffle of the safeguard file** — `chunker_oracle_safeguard.rs` was added, renamed away, then re-introduced as a zero-test stub.  Each step has a clear, well-documented rationale in its commit body (oracle `git stash push -- <path>` fataling on unknown paths after a rename).  The net effect on the tree is two files: one real guard (`chunker_public_api_guard.rs`) and one 21-line placeholder (`chunker_oracle_safeguard.rs`).  Not a blocker; flagged because the reshuffle is visible in `git log` and the verifier/user may wish to understand why.
